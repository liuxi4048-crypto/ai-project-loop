The Antikythera mechanism is an ancient Greek analog device used to predict
astronomical positions and eclipses decades in advance. Discovered in 1901 in a
shipwreck off the Greek island of Antikythera, it consists of a complex system
of at least thirty bronze gears. Researchers believe it was built around the
second century BC. The device could track the movements of the Sun and the Moon
through the zodiac and predict the timing of eclipses using the Saros cycle.
